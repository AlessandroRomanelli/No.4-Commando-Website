About Us

Who were the real No.4 Commandos?

The British Commandos were formed during the Second World War in June 1940, following a request from the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, Winston Churchill, for a force that could carry out hard hitting attacks on German positions on any front.

What is this unit about?

Training in the Scottish Highlands to beach landings in Normandy to Norway, ambushing enemies using iconic gear, weapons and vehicles provided by the mods we use as well as our own exclusive content. Sniper nests in clock towers to concrete pillbox's on open fields with para-drops into flak filled skies. The missions and scenarios are endless and we aim to replicate the conflict as closely to real life as possible whilst also keeping the relaxed aspect.

What makes us stick out from all the other units?

We take great pride to define ourselves as the old-school people. The ones that prefer to see the enemy directly through the iron sight of our bolt-action rifles, rather than through some kind of high-power magnfication lens attached to an automatic rifle. We face Death from up close on the battlefield and yet its threat and menace finds and shall find us unafraid. Doesn't matter how dirty, how dangerous the business, we do it and we do it well.

Weapons

Lee-Enfield Mk.IV

The Mk.IV Lee Enfield is the standard issued rifle that every soldier is trained with in the British Army. It packs .303 cartridges which can knock out unconscious (if not kill) the enemy with just one accurate shot. It was first introduced in 1895 and retired in 1957, which says a lot about how good and versatile this rifle was. If you find yourself against a highly trained soldier carrying the Enfield, you are about to have a bad day. However it doesn't lack some disadvantages: the light weight makes for a great kickback for every shot you take. Moreover it only fits ten rounds at a time and it's bolt action, which make it a not so reliable weapon for close quarters combat. It has a good effective firing range.

Sten Gun (SMG)

The Sten Gun is the British solution to the lack of fire rate of the Lee Enfield, it chambers 9mm Parabellum rounds and it's capable of gunning down an entire squad at close range if its 32 rounds magazine is emptied carefully. The high fire rate comes at a price and that is the lack of range, limited to about 100m and stopping power: it will take several shots to knock-out or kill the opponent. A variant of the Sten with a suppressor is also available. Anti-Tank riflemen and Sappers are usually issued with this rifle since they are the most likely to fight the enemy at close range.

Bren Gun (LMG)

The Bren Light Machine Gun is one of the first of its kind to make it's appearance between the allies: capable of firing up to 500 .303 rounds per minute, its power is devastating and it is feared throughout the German lines. It normally fits 30 rounds magazines that get emptied quite fast. It's an incredible gun to inflict some suppression fire, useful to provide cover for your allies that need to move. The major downsides are weight related: the weapon itself is kind of heavy but most of the weight comes from the huge amount of magazines that the autorifleman has to carry, therefore it's almost always helped out by an autorifleman assistant, or ammobearer.

M1 Rocket Launcher (Bazooka)

There is nothing more fearful that a Pz.Kpfw III coming towards you with the only intent to wipe you and your whole section out. That's when the M1 Bazooka comes into play. A trained anti-tank rifleman can take down an incautious tank with a single aimed shot, making it the perfect tool for ambushes and the defense from armored assaults. Its rockets are faster than any other rocket-launcher available, making it the most accurate and giving it an effective shooting range of 300m. Because the M1 Bazooka is so heavy and many rockets might be required during a sortie, the AT rifleman is usually teamed with an ammobearer that can carry rockets for him.

Explosives

When bullets and rockets simply aren't enough, there come the Sappers with their charges, ready to blow up anything that crosses Commando's path! The explosive used by the Commandos are mostly TNT based and come in different sizes: 250g, 1kg, 2kg. Depending on the target, the right charge is used and through the use of a detonator the object is blown up from a short but safe distance. It's an incredibly effective mean of initiating an ambush since anti-tank and anti-personnel mines fall into this category. Sappers can be highly versatile by carrying different kind of charges. The charges are usually spreaded among the group to avoid the Sapper being too heavy and can be placed down by anyone but the Sapper is the only one that can trigger them.
